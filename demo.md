# Aftercall — Submission Video (≤60s)

The **submission video** is a *separate artifact* from the 90-second stage script
(see [`docs/DEMO.md`](docs/DEMO.md)). It's a tight, captioned, hard-cut screen capture
that **reads on mute** — captions carry every Dial primitive so judges scoring
without sound still see the depth. Record during the freeze hour, not at the
buzzer.

This video opens with a **narrative cold open**: problem → agitate → the flip.

## The core insight the hook is built on

Aftercall is **outbound**. The old way is **inbound**. A victim dialing a jammed
hotline and getting no answer isn't just sad — it's the *exact failure we invert*.
Don't make them call. **We call them. All of them. At once.** The hook dramatizes
the inbound failure, then flips the arrow. That arrow-flip is the wow.

## Must-have vs. cut-if-short

- **Must-have (3 shots):** the jammed-line problem (0:00–0:05), the outbound
  cascade with real Hebrew audio (0:13–0:18), the red escalation call
  (0:30–0:42). These carry impact + Dial depth + human-in-the-loop.
- **Cut first if over 60s:** trim the agitate beat (0:05–0:10) to 3 seconds. The
  narrative costs ~12–15s of budget — protect the back half, because judges
  reward *visible Dial depth* over production polish.

## Alternate cold-open lines (same visuals)

- **Stakes/human:** "In the first hour after a disaster, the difference between OK
  and not-OK is a phone call nobody has time to make — *times ten thousand.*"
- **Provocation (phone-native):** "A blackout takes out every app on this screen.
  It doesn't take out a phone call." — sharpest single sentence we have; good as a
  mid-video punch even if we open with the narrative.

## Recording checklist

- **Reads on mute.** Every Dial primitive (outbound triage, escalation call,
  inbound/outbound SMS, webhook-driven dots) must be legible from captions alone.
- **Make the number huge.** "10,000 calls" on screen, large — the wow is the
  *parallelism*, not that it dials. A wall of dots flipping at once is the frame
  they remember.
- **Real audio once.** One genuine Hebrew Dial exchange on the cascade beat;
  pre-record a clean backup clip in case the network dies during capture.
- **Hard cuts + captions, no narration track.** Public Loom/YouTube/Vimeo link.
- **Freeze-hour discipline:** storyboard locked before recording; one good take of
  each must-have shot beats ten loose ones.
